What do we do?

We bring fresh energy and new perspectives to conservation projects, helping them reach their peak impact in the real world.

We support ecological and social surveys, experiments, GIS mapping, data analysis, evidence reviews, grant writing, programme development, capacity building, and more.

Who can we help?

We work with NGOs, government agencies, research institutions, and projects across environmental research, nature conservation, and international development.

We can fill gaps by completing specific pieces of work, or bring broader scientific support and perspective when a project needs extra capacity.

Why work with us?

  • Rigorous and relevant: strong scientific standards, focused on useful outcomes.
  • Flexible and practical: use us as much or as little as the project needs.
  • Good to work with: collaborative, thoughtful, and ready to help projects get moving.

Meet Apex

Apex is led by Dr Ben Phillips and Dr Emily Duncan — two conservation scientists combining ecological expertise, social research, data skills, and a collaborative approach.

Dr Ben Phillips

Ben is a nature conservation scientist with over 10 years' experience delivering applied research that supports biodiversity, informs land management, and connects people with nature.

His work combines ecological and social science, with particular experience in pollinator conservation, road verge management, and understanding people's attitudes and behaviours relating to nature.

Dr Emily Duncan

Emily is a marine conservation scientist with more than a decade of experience carrying out research on marine megafauna, plastic pollution, and international conservation projects.

She is experienced in large international projects, capacity building, community engagement, and currently sits on the expert panel for Defra's Ocean Community Empowerment and Nature Grants Programme.
