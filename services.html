Detailed services

A closer look at what we do.

The examples below show the kinds of work we provide, from focused pieces of research and analysis to wider scientific input for project development and delivery.

1 Ecology and conservation

We design and deliver ecological research that helps organisations understand species, habitats and pressures on nature, across both land and ocean systems.

Our experience includes:

  • Field research and ecological studies. Surveys, experiments and applied research designed around clear conservation questions.
  • Habitats and land management. Evidence-based work relating to pollinators, road verges, public green spaces, farmland and habitat management.
  • Marine conservation. Research on marine megafauna, plastic pollution, fisheries waste and conservation challenges in island and coastal settings.

2 Social research

We use social research to understand how people think about, value and interact with nature, helping conservation become more effective, inclusive and grounded in real experience.

We can support:

  • Surveys and questionnaires. Designing, analysing and interpreting surveys on public attitudes, behaviours and relationships with nature.
  • Interviews and focus groups. Gathering insight from stakeholders, communities and practitioners to inform conservation planning and strategy.
  • Mixed data sources. Combining survey responses, social media and other digital data to understand how people engage with nature.

3 Data and spatial analysis

We help organisations make sense of ecological, environmental and social data, turning complex datasets into clear evidence, maps and practical insight.

We can support:

  • Spatial analysis and mapping. GIS, remote assessment and mapping approaches to understand habitats, pressures and patterns across landscapes.
  • Data analysis. Working with ecological, environmental and social datasets to answer applied conservation questions.
  • Accessible outputs. Clear figures, maps, summaries and interpretation that help evidence reach the people who need to use it.

4 Evidence synthesis

We review and synthesise scientific evidence so organisations can make better use of existing knowledge and understand what it means for conservation practice, policy or funding.

We can support:

  • Evidence reviews. Finding, assessing and summarising evidence from papers, reports, datasets and expert knowledge.
  • Applied interpretation. Drawing out what the evidence means for decisions, proposals, programmes and practical next steps.
  • Clear outputs. Producing summaries, reports and recommendations that are useful for planning, policy, funding and communication.

5 Programme development

We provide scientific input for proposals, partnerships and programmes, helping organisations turn strong conservation ideas into fundable, deliverable work.

We can support:

  • Grant writing and proposals. Clear applications with strong evidence, realistic methods and a convincing conservation case.
  • Programme design. Scientific input for planning, delivery, monitoring, evaluation and learning.
  • Partnership working. Support for collaborative projects, capacity building and community-focused environmental initiatives.

Need something else?
