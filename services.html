Our services

Every project is different, so we tailor our support to fit the context, the team, the available evidence, and the decisions that need to be made. Below are the main ways we can help.

1. Ecological Services

Understanding habitats, species, and practical management options

We offer support with ecological research — from field surveys and experimental studies to evidence-based advice on habitat management across terrestrial and marine environments.

Our experience covers:

  • Ecological studies — designing and carrying out surveys and experiments to answer applied ecological questions.
  • Habitat and land management — with particular expertise in road verges, public green spaces, farmland, and pollinator habitats.
  • Marine conservation — including marine megafauna conservation, fisheries waste, and monitoring of plastic pollution.

2. Social Science Approaches

Understanding how people think, feel, and act in relation to nature

By exploring people's attitudes, motivations, values, and behaviours, we can help shape more effective, inclusive, and realistic conservation strategies.

We have experience with a range of social science methods:

  • Questionnaire design — including developing surveys of 18,000 adults across England with Natural England, and public surveys relating to pollinators.
  • Stakeholder interviews & focus groups — including work to guide the development of a conservation strategy for hazel dormouse.
  • Analysis of digital data sources — including integrating survey data, social media, and other digital sources to understand how people engage with nature.

3. Evidence Synthesis

Making sense of the evidence so decisions can move forward

We are experienced in synthesising evidence to a rigorous scientific standard — from horizon scanning to systematic reviews — helping organisations make informed decisions based on the best available knowledge.

Our work has reviewed and summarised evidence on topics including:

  • Environmental links between forests and oceans for The Woodland Trust.
  • Interactions between marine plastic pollution and turtles / other megafauna.
  • Enhancing road verges for pollinators and broader ecosystem services.

4. Data Services

Turning complex data into useful insight

We can help with managing, visualising, and analysing ecological and social data — transforming complex or messy datasets into meaningful insights that support effective decision-making.

Our expertise includes spatial mapping and analysis using Geographical Information Systems (GIS), digital data sources, and the handling of large environmental datasets.

Examples of our past work include:

  • Remotely assessing road verge habitats using Google Earth and Street View.
  • Modelling road pollution across Great Britain.
  • Comparing light pollution levels across UK local authorities and habitats.

5. Programme Development

Helping conservation projects get designed, funded, and delivered

We can provide scientific support across all stages of grant writing and programme development — from shaping early ideas to building robust, fundable proposals and supporting successful project delivery.

Our experience includes:

  • Grant writing — with experience as both applicants and reviewers, helping craft clear, compelling proposals that resonate with funders.
  • Project management — supporting the design, coordination, delivery, monitoring, evaluation, learning, and reporting of scientific programmes.
  • Capacity building & community engagement — including work focused on gender, equality, and social inclusion in environmental initiatives.

6. Other Services
