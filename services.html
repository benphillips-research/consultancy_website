Our services

Every project is different, so we tailor our support to fit the context, the team, the available evidence, and the decisions that need to be made.

1. Ecological Services

Understanding habitats, species, and practical management options

We support ecological research across terrestrial and marine environments. This can include field surveys, experimental studies, evidence reviews, and advice on habitat management.

Our experience includes:

  • Ecological studies: designing and carrying out surveys and experiments to answer applied ecological questions.
  • Habitat and land management: work relating to road verges, public green spaces, farmland, and pollinator habitats.
  • Marine conservation: experience with marine megafauna, fisheries waste, and monitoring of plastic pollution.

2. Social Science Approaches

Understanding how people think, feel, and act in relation to nature

Conservation depends on people as well as ecology. We can help explore attitudes, motivations, values, behaviours, and relationships with nature, then use that insight to shape more effective and inclusive strategies.

Our experience includes:

  • Questionnaire design: including surveys of 18,000 adults across England with Natural England, and public surveys relating to pollinators.
  • Stakeholder interviews and focus groups: including work to guide a conservation strategy for hazel dormouse.
  • Digital data sources: including work using survey data, social media, and other digital sources to understand how people engage with nature.

3. Evidence Synthesis

Making sense of evidence so decisions can move forward

We are experienced in reviewing and synthesising evidence to a rigorous scientific standard. This can help organisations understand complex topics, identify knowledge gaps, and make better-informed decisions.

Our work has reviewed and summarised evidence on topics including:

  • Environmental links between forests and oceans for The Woodland Trust.
  • Interactions between marine plastic pollution and turtles and other megafauna.
  • Enhancing road verges for pollinators and broader ecosystem services.

4. Data Services

Turning complex data into useful insight

We can help manage, visualise, and analyse ecological and social data. Our aim is to turn complex or messy datasets into clear insight that supports practical decision-making.

Our expertise includes spatial mapping and analysis using Geographical Information Systems (GIS), digital data sources, and large environmental datasets.

Examples of our past work include:

  • Remotely assessing road verge habitats using Google Earth and Street View.
  • Modelling road pollution across Great Britain.
  • Comparing light pollution levels across UK local authorities and habitats.

5. Programme Development

Helping conservation projects get designed, funded, and delivered

We can provide scientific support across grant writing and programme development. This might mean shaping an early idea, strengthening a proposal, or supporting project delivery once funding is secured.

Our experience includes:

  • Grant writing: experience as applicants and reviewers, helping to craft clear and compelling proposals.
  • Project support: design, coordination, delivery, monitoring, evaluation, learning, and reporting.
  • Capacity building and community engagement: including work focused on gender, equality, and social inclusion in environmental initiatives.

6. Other Services

Need something else?

If your project needs help with something not listed here, please get in touch. We have broad experience across conservation research and project delivery, and we may be able to help you find the right next step.
